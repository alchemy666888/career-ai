AI Job Search User Guide for Job Hunters

This guide explains how to organize your search, choose stronger roles, prepare applications, and track every opportunity from discovery to outcome.

Key concepts

Pipeline

The pipeline is your job-search board. Each role belongs to one stage:

DiscoveredYou found the role but have not decided whether to pursue it.
ShortlistedYou saved the role because it is worth deeper review.
ApplyingYou are preparing documents and checking requirements.
SubmittedYou submitted externally and marked it submitted in the app.
InterviewingYou are preparing for or tracking interviews.
OfferYou received an offer or are discussing final details.
AcceptedYou accepted the role.
ClosedThe role is no longer active because it was rejected, withdrawn, expired, declined, or otherwise closed.

Shortlist

Your Shortlist is the set of roles you saved because they deserve attention. Use it to avoid losing promising opportunities in a long list of job posts.

Application workspace

An application workspace is where you prepare for one role. The app does not submit applications for you; submit on the employer site, then mark the role as submitted.

Evidence and fit

Evidence is information you approve from your profile, résumé, or corrections. Fit evaluation uses that evidence to explain strengths, gaps, and confidence.

Recommended workflow

1. Start with your profile

  1. Open Profile.
  2. Add target roles, preferred locations, skills, and background.
  3. Review extracted résumé claims if résumé import is available.
  4. Correct inaccurate or incomplete imported claims.
  5. Keep strong stories and measurable outcomes easy to reuse.

2. Discover roles

  1. Open Discover or /jobs.
  2. Search by keyword, role, company, location, or work style.
  3. Review the role description, source, freshness, and requirements.
  4. Save promising roles and dismiss obvious mismatches.

3. Decide what to shortlist

Shortlist a role when it matches your target, has acceptable logistics, includes interesting responsibilities, and gives you enough evidence to apply well.

4. Prepare an application

  1. Open the shortlisted role.
  2. Start or open its application workspace.
  3. Tailor your résumé and cover letter.
  4. Complete the preflight checklist.
  5. Submit on the employer site or job board.
  6. Return to AI Job Search and mark the application as Submitted.

Application preflight checklist

  • Your name and contact details are correct.
  • Your résumé is targeted to the role.
  • Your cover letter names the right company and role.
  • Portfolio, LinkedIn, GitHub, or website links work.
  • Required questions are answered truthfully.
  • No unsupported claim was added by mistake.
  • You saved a copy of what you submitted.

5. Follow up after submission

Record submission date, source, confirmation details, follow-up target, and next action.

Submitted on: YYYY-MM-DD
Source: Company careers page / referral / job board
Confirmation: Email received / portal status / none
Follow-up target: YYYY-MM-DD
Next action: Prepare recruiter screen examples

6. Prepare for interviews

Review the job description, identify top requirements, choose proof stories, practice answers, prepare questions, and record signals after each conversation.

7. Manage offers

Compare compensation, benefits, work style, location, manager fit, team health, learning potential, and risk before accepting or declining.

8. Close roles cleanly

Use Closed for rejected, withdrawn, expired, declined, or no-longer-relevant opportunities. Add notes so you can learn from the search.

Practical weekly routine

CadenceActions
Daily, 15–30 minutesReview discovered roles, save or dismiss roles, update active notes, respond to recruiter activity.
Twice per week, 45–90 minutesPrepare and submit selected applications, tailor documents, follow up on submissions.
Weekly, 30 minutesCount roles by pipeline stage, remove stale roles, identify bottlenecks, choose next deep-work targets.

Tips for better outcomes

  • Apply selectively instead of sending generic applications everywhere.
  • Keep your Shortlist small enough to act on.
  • Write notes immediately after interviews.
  • Use evidence and specific examples in every document.
  • Do not mark an application submitted until it is actually submitted externally.
  • Keep closed opportunities for learning, not clutter.

Privacy and control reminders

  • Review all generated or suggested text before using it.
  • Do not include sensitive information unless necessary.
  • The app is a preparation and tracking tool, not a promise of employment.
  • You remain responsible for external submissions, employer communications, and final decisions.
